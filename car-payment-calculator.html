How Car Payments Are Calculated in 2026

Your monthly car payment depends on the loan amount, interest rate, and loan term. The loan amount is calculated as:

  • Loan Amount = Vehicle Price − Down Payment − Trade-in Value + Sales Tax on (Price − Trade-in)

Example: $35,000 car − $7,000 down − $0 trade-in + 6% tax on $35,000 = $30,100 financed. At 6.9% for 60 months = $595/month.

Average Car Payment in 2026

  • New car average: $726/month (loan amount ~$40,000)
  • Used car average: $533/month (loan amount ~$28,000)
  • Average loan term: 69 months for new, 67 months for used
  • Average APR: 6.6% new, 11.2% used
  • % of vehicles financed: 82% new, 70% used

2026 Car Loan Rates by Term

  • 24 months: 5.5-6.5% new / 6.5-8% used
  • 36 months: 5.8-6.8% new / 7-8.5% used
  • 48 months: 6.0-7.0% new / 7.5-9% used
  • 60 months: 6.5-7.5% new / 8-10% used
  • 72 months: 7.0-8.0% new / 9-11% used
  • 84 months: 7.5-9.0% new / 10-13% used

Rule of thumb: Shorter term = lower rate + less total interest. Choose the shortest term you can comfortably afford.

Down Payment: How Much Do You Need?

A larger down payment means lower monthly payments and less total interest:

  • $0 down on $35K @ 6.9%, 60mo: $692/month, $6,527 interest
  • $5,000 down: $593/month, $5,594 interest — save $930
  • $7,000 down (20%): $558/month, $5,263 interest — save $1,261
  • $10,000 down: $494/month, $4,663 interest — save $1,861

20% down on new, 10% on used is recommended to avoid being upside-down on the loan.

Trade-in vs Private Sale

  • Trade-in: Easier and faster; dealer handles paperwork. In most states, you only pay sales tax on the difference (price − trade-in), which can save $1,000-2,000
  • Private sale: Typically gets $1,000-3,000 more than trade-in value. But requires time, effort, and dealing with strangers
  • Best approach: Get trade-in quote from dealer, then try private sale. Sell privately if you can beat the offer by $1,500+

The True Cost of Owning a Car (Beyond the Payment)

Your monthly car payment is only part of the cost. The average American spends $12,000+/year on car ownership:

  • Car payment: $500-726/month
  • Insurance: $150-250/month
  • Gas: $100-200/month
  • Maintenance & repairs: $50-100/month
  • Registration & fees: $10-30/month
  • Depreciation: 15-25% first year, 10-15% per year after
  • Parking: $0-300/month depending on city

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the average car payment in 2026?

The average new car payment is $726/month and the average used car payment is $533/month. Average loan amounts are $40,000 for new cars and $28,000 for used cars. The average loan term is about 69 months.

How much should I put down on a car?

Recommended: 20% down on a new car, 10% on a used car. On a $35,000 new car, that's $7,000 down. A larger down payment means a lower monthly payment, less total interest, and avoids being "upside down" on the loan.

Is a 72-month car loan a good idea?

Generally no. While a 72-month loan lowers your monthly payment, you'll pay significantly more in interest and likely be "upside down" (owing more than the car is worth) for years. Aim for 48-60 months maximum. If you need 72+ months to afford the payment, the car may be too expensive for your budget.

Should I trade in my car or sell it privately?

Private sale typically gets $1,000-3,000 more than trade-in value. However, trade-in is faster and the tax benefit (you only pay sales tax on the difference) can offset some of the loss. Get both quotes before deciding.

How is a car payment calculated?

Car payment uses the amortization formula: Monthly Payment = (Loan Amount × Monthly Rate) / (1 - (1 + Monthly Rate)^(-Number of Months)). Loan amount = vehicle price minus down payment minus trade-in, plus sales tax. Use our calculator for your exact numbers.
