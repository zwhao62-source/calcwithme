Fraction Calculator

Add, subtract, multiply, or divide two fractions and see the result in simplified form. Fractions are written as a/b where a is the numerator and b is the denominator. This calculator shows step-by-step working.

How to Add Fractions

To add fractions, find a common denominator (usually the LCM of the two denominators), convert both fractions, then add the numerators. Example: 1/2 + 1/3 = 3/6 + 2/6 = 5/6.

How to Multiply Fractions

Multiply the numerators together and the denominators together. Example: (1/2) × (3/4) = 3/8. Simplify by dividing both by their GCD.

Simplifying Fractions

A fraction is simplified when the numerator and denominator have no common factors other than 1. Divide both by their greatest common divisor (GCD). For example, 6/8 simplifies to 3/4 (divide both by 2).
