The True Cost of Car Ownership in 2026

Most people underestimate how much car ownership really costs. According to AAA's 2026 Driving Your Costs study, the average American spends $10,728 per year on car ownership. A $35,000 new car can cost $50,000–$60,000 over 5 years when you include all expenses.

2026 Average Car Ownership Costs by Category

  • Loan Payments: $5,400/yr ($450/mo for $35K car with 5-year loan)
  • Gas/Fuel: $2,400/yr ($200/mo at 12,000 miles/yr)
  • Insurance: $1,800/yr ($150/mo for full coverage)
  • Maintenance & Repairs: $1,200/yr ($100/mo average)
  • Parking & Tolls: $600–$2,400/yr (varies by city)
  • Depreciation: $4,000–$7,000/yr (the hidden cost!)
  • Registration & Taxes: $300–$600/yr

Depreciation: The Biggest Hidden Cost

Depreciation is the largest expense most drivers never think about:

  • Year 1: Lose 20–30% of value (new $35K car → $25K)
  • Year 2–3: Lose another 15–20%
  • Year 4–5: Lose another 10–15%
  • 5-year total: ~50–60% depreciation

Tip: Buying a 3-year-old used car can save you 40–50% on depreciation alone.

Cost per Mile by Vehicle Type (2026 Estimates)

Breaking Down Car Ownership Costs

  • Loan Payments: Your monthly payment to the lender
  • Gas/Fuel: Ongoing fuel costs based on driving habits
  • Insurance: Required auto insurance (varies by driver)
  • Maintenance: Oil changes, tires, repairs - typically $0.05-0.10/mile
  • Parking & Tolls: Can range from $0 to $500+/month in major cities
  • Depreciation: The biggest hidden cost - cars lose 20-30% value in year 1

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the true cost of owning a car?

Beyond the purchase price, true car costs include loan payments, gas, insurance, maintenance, repairs, parking, registration, and depreciation. Most people underestimate these costs by 30-50%. A $35,000 car can cost $50,000+ over 5 years when all factors are included.

How much does depreciation affect car ownership cost?

Depreciation is often the largest hidden cost. A new car loses 20-30% of its value in the first year alone. Over 5 years, depreciation typically accounts for 40-50% of the total cost of ownership for a new vehicle.

What is a reasonable cost per mile for car ownership?

A reasonable range is $0.50-1.00 per mile for a moderately-priced vehicle. This includes loan payments, gas, insurance, maintenance, and depreciation. Electric vehicles may have lower fuel and maintenance costs but similar depreciation.

How often should I budget for major car maintenance?

Budget approximately $0.05-0.10 per mile for maintenance and repairs. This covers oil changes, tires, brakes, and unexpected repairs. Major expenses like transmission or engine work typically occur after 100,000+ miles.
