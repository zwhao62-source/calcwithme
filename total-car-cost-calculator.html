The True Cost of Car Ownership

Most people underestimate how much car ownership really costs. Beyond the monthly loan payment, there are gas, insurance, maintenance, parking, and most importantly - depreciation.

Breaking Down Car Ownership Costs

  • Loan Payments: Your monthly payment to the lender
  • Gas/Fuel: Ongoing fuel costs based on driving habits
  • Insurance: Required auto insurance (varies by driver)
  • Maintenance: Oil changes, tires, repairs - typically $0.05-0.10/mile
  • Parking & Tolls: Can range from $0 to $500+/month in major cities
  • Depreciation: The biggest hidden cost - cars lose 20-30% value in year 1

Cost per Mile Matters

A $35,000 car driven 12,000 miles per year for 5 years costs about $0.58 per mile in loan payments alone. Add operating costs and depreciation, and the true cost can exceed $1.00 per mile.
