DIY Truck Rental Costs

  • U-Haul: $19.95/day + $0.79/mile (in-town); one-way rates $300-$3,000 depending on distance and truck size
  • Penske: $29.99/day + $0.79/mile; often has newer trucks and better insurance options
  • Budget: $29.99/day + $0.75/mile; typically 10-15% cheaper than U-Haul for one-way moves
  • Enterprise: $89+/day for cargo vans; better for small loads
  • Gas cost: Budget $50-$200 for local moves; $100-$500 for long-distance (trucks get 8-12 MPG)
  • Insurance: $15-$50/day for damage coverage (highly recommended — you're liable for any damage)
  • Dolly + pads: $20-$40/day for appliance dolly; $10-$25/dozen for furniture pads

Professional Movers Costs

Full-service movers charge by the hour for local moves and by weight/distance for long-distance:

  • Local hourly rate: $80-$120/hour for 2 movers + truck; $120-$180/hour for 3-4 movers
  • Long-distance: $0.50-$1.00/pound + $0.50-$1.00/mile (a 3-bedroom home, ~8,000 lbs, costs $2,500-$6,000 for 500 miles)
  • Specialty items: Pianos $200-$500; pool tables $300-$600; artwork/safes $150-$400 extra
  • Stairs/elevator: $50-$200 extra per flight or if no elevator access
  • Long carry: $75-$150 extra if truck can't park within 50-75 feet of your door

Packing Supplies & Services

  • Boxes: $1.50-$4.00 each (buy used on Facebook Marketplace for $0.50-$1.00)
  • Tape: $3-$6/roll (need 2-4 rolls per room)
  • Bubble wrap: $15-$25/roll
  • Packing paper: $10-$20/bundle
  • Full packing service: $300-$800 for a 2-bedroom home; $500-$1,500 for a 3-4 bedroom
  • Fragile-only packing: $150-$400 (electronics, dishes, artwork)

Hidden Moving Costs to Budget For

  • Security deposit + first month's rent: At your new place (if renting)
  • Utility setup fees: $50-$200 for electricity, gas, water, internet activation
  • Cleaning service: $150-$400 to clean your old place (required by many leases)
  • Hotel + meals: $150-$300/night for multi-day long-distance moves
  • Storage: $50-$300/month if there's a gap between move-out and move-in
  • Tips for movers: $20-$50/person for local; $50-$100/person for long-distance (not required but appreciated)
  • Change of address: $1.25 for USPS; update driver's license, car registration, voter registration (fees vary by state)

Money-Saving Moving Tips

  • Declutter first: Sell or donate 20-30% of your stuff — every pound costs money to move
  • Move during off-peak: Weekdays and mid-month are 10-20% cheaper than weekends and month-end
  • Get 3+ quotes: Movers' prices vary by 30-50% for the same job — always compare
  • Use free boxes: Liquor stores, grocery stores, and Facebook Marketplace have free boxes
  • Ask about price matching: Many movers will match a competitor's written quote
  • Tax deduction: If you're moving for work 50+ miles farther from your old home, moving costs may be tax-deductible (check with a tax pro)

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to move locally?

Local moves (under 100 miles) typically cost $300-$1,500 for a 2-3 bedroom home using a rental truck (DIY), or $800-$2,500 with professional movers. The biggest cost factor is home size and whether you hire help.

How much does a long-distance move cost?

Long-distance moves (over 100 miles) cost $1,500-$6,000 for a 1-2 bedroom home using a rental truck (plus gas and lodging), or $2,500-$8,000 with professional movers. Cross-country moves can exceed $10,000 with full service.

Is it cheaper to buy new furniture than move?

For studio/1-bedroom moves over 500+ miles, sometimes yes — especially for low-quality furniture. A one-bedroom move cross-country costs $1,500-$3,000, while replacing basic furniture might cost $2,000-$4,000. Get quotes both ways.
