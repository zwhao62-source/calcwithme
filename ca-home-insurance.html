California Home Insurance Overview

California homeowners insurance averages $1,980/year ($165/month), which is near the national average of $2,230/year. California rates are driven by wildfire risk, though rates are partially regulated.

  • Average annual premium: $1,980
  • Average monthly: $165
  • Per $1,000 coverage: $3.14
  • Median home value: $631,200
  • California rank: #28 most expensive state

What California Home Insurance Covers

  • Dwelling: Physical structure of your home and attached structures
  • Other structures: Detached garage, shed, fence (typically 10% of dwelling coverage)
  • Personal property: Furniture, electronics, clothing (50-70% of dwelling)
  • Loss of use: Hotel and living expenses if home is uninhabitable (20-30% of dwelling)
  • Liability: Legal protection if someone is injured on your property ($100K-$300K standard)
  • Medical payments: Small medical bills for guests injured on your property ($1K-$5K)

California-Specific Insurance Risks

  • Wildfire: High risk in many areas — FAIR Plan available if insurers won't cover you
  • Earthquake: Not covered by standard insurance — separate earthquake policy needed
  • Landslide: Often excluded — check your policy carefully

How to Save on California Home Insurance

  • Bundle home + auto: Save 10-25% on both policies with the same insurer
  • Raise your deductible: Going from $500 to $2,000 deductible can save 15-25%
  • Improve home security: Alarm systems, deadbolts, and smoke detectors save 5-15%
  • Maintain good credit: In most states, credit score impacts insurance rates significantly
  • Stay claim-free: 3-5 years without a claim earns a 10-20% discount
  • Shop every 2 years: Rates change constantly. Get 3-5 quotes each renewal cycle
  • Ask about California discounts: New roof, impact-resistant roofing, wind mitigation, loyalty

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is home insurance in California?

Average home insurance in California costs $1,980/year or $165/month. That's $3.14 per $1,000 of coverage. Rates vary by location, home age, and coverage level.

Is home insurance required in California?

California law does not require homeowners insurance, but your mortgage lender will require it. Even without a mortgage, insurance protects your largest asset from fire, storms, theft, and liability.
