Arizona First-Time Home Buyer Overview

Arizona offers several programs through Arizona Department of Housing (ADOH) to help first-time buyers achieve homeownership:

  • Median home price in Arizona: $353,500
  • Minimum down payment: 5% ($17,675 on median home)
  • Down payment assistance: Up to 5% of purchase price
  • DPA interest rate: 0% (forgiven after 5-15 years)
  • Maximum income limit: $105,000
  • Estimated monthly P&I: $2,101 (on median home with 6.4% rate)

Arizona Down Payment Assistance Programs

The primary source of first-time buyer assistance in Arizona is Arizona Department of Housing (ADOH). Key benefits:

  • Down payment assistance: Up to 5% of purchase price
  • Repayment terms: 0% (forgiven after 5-15 years)
  • Income limit: $105,000
  • Credit score minimum: Typically 620-640 (varies by program)
  • First-time buyer definition: Haven't owned a primary residence in the past 3 years
  • Homebuyer education: Required — typically a free online course

How to Buy a Home in Arizona with Little Money Down

  • Step 1: Check eligibility — Contact Arizona Department of Housing (ADOH) to verify income limits and program availability
  • Step 2: Get pre-approved — Work with an approved lender who offers Arizona bond programs
  • Step 3: Complete homebuyer education — Most Arizona programs require a HUD-approved counseling course
  • Step 4: Apply for DPA — Your lender submits the down payment assistance application with your mortgage
  • Step 5: Close on your home — DPA funds are applied at closing, reducing your out-of-pocket costs

Types of Arizona First-Time Buyer Programs

  • Down payment grants: Free money that never needs to be repaid. Typically $5,000-15,000. Available in Arizona through federal HOME/SHOP funds
  • Forgivable loans: 0% interest loans forgiven after 5-15 years. Arizona's primary DPA program uses this structure (0% (forgiven after 5-15 years))
  • Deferred loans: 0% interest, no payments until you sell, refinance, or pay off the first mortgage
  • Below-market rate mortgages: First mortgages at 0.25-0.5% below market rate, saving $11+/month
  • MCC (Mortgage Credit Certificate): Federal tax credit of 20-30% of mortgage interest — worth $1,500-3,000/year

Arizona First-Time Buyer Income Limits

Most Arizona programs have income limits to ensure assistance goes to those who need it:

  • Maximum income: $105,000 (varies by household size and county)
  • Higher limits may apply in targeted areas (census tracts with lower homeownership rates)
  • Income types counted: All household members' gross income from all sources
  • Exceptions: Some programs have no income limits in targeted areas

Frequently Overlooked Arizona Programs

  • Good Neighbor Next Door: 50% off home price for teachers, firefighters, EMTs, police in revitalization areas
  • HUD $1 Homes: Foreclosed HUD homes sold at $1 to qualifying nonprofits and government agencies
  • Section 8 Homeownership: Use your Section 8 voucher toward mortgage payments instead of rent
  • Native American programs: Section 184 loans with 1.25% down for eligible tribal members
  • Employer assistance: Some Arizona employers offer homebuyer grants — check with HR
