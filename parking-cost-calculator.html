Parking Cost Calculator — 2026 Updated Rates

Parking is one of the most overlooked costs of car ownership. In 2026, the average American commuter spends $150–$300 per month on parking, which adds up to $1,800–$3,600 per year. Our free parking cost calculator uses real 2026 data to help you estimate expenses accurately and find savings.

2026 Average Parking Costs by City

Parking rates vary dramatically by location. Here are the latest average monthly parking costs for major US cities in 2026:

  • New York City: $500–$700/month (Manhattan can exceed $800)
  • San Francisco: $350–$450/month
  • Boston: $300–$400/month
  • Chicago: $250–$350/month
  • Seattle: $200–$300/month
  • Denver: $150–$250/month

  • Portland: $120–$200/month
  • Nashville: $100–$180/month

Monthly vs Daily Parking: Which Is Cheaper?

If you commute 5 days a week, a monthly parking pass is almost always cheaper than paying daily rates. Here's a comparison:

  • Daily rate of $15/day × 22 work days = $330/month
  • Monthly pass average = $200/month (39% savings!)
  • Annual savings with a monthly pass = $1,560/year

Use our calculator above to compare your specific situation. Enter your daily rate and the monthly pass discount to see exactly how much you'd save.

Hidden Parking Costs Most People Forget

  • Event parking: Sports games, concerts — $20–$50 per event
  • Weekend parking: Dining out, shopping — $10–$30 per weekend
  • Parking tickets: The average US driver gets 1–2 tickets per year ($50–$150 each)
  • Guest parking: Apartment buildings charge $25–$75/month for extra spots
  • EV charging parking: Some stations charge idle fees of $0.40/min after charging

5 Proven Ways to Save on Parking in 2026

  1. Use parking apps — SpotHero, ParkWhiz, and ParkMobile offer 20–50% off regular rates
  2. Park further away and walk — Rates drop significantly just 2–3 blocks from downtown
  3. Negotiate your monthly rate — Many garages offer unadvertised discounts for long-term commitments
  4. Consider public transit — In most major cities, a transit pass ($70–$130/month) costs less than parking
  5. Car-share instead of own — Zipcar, Turo eliminate parking costs entirely

Frequently Asked Questions

How much should I budget for parking?

Parking costs vary widely by location. Small cities: $40-80/month, medium cities: $100-150/month, large metros: $175-250/month, and premium downtown areas: $300-500+/month. Use our calculator to estimate your specific situation.

Is a monthly parking pass worth it?

Yes, in most cases. Monthly passes typically offer 20-40% savings compared to daily rates. If you park in the same location regularly, a monthly pass almost always saves money and eliminates the hassle of daily payment.

How do I calculate my annual parking costs?

Multiply your average daily parking rate by your work days per week, then by 52 weeks. Add any monthly passes, event parking, and weekend expenses. Our calculator does this automatically for you.

What is the average parking cost in major US cities?

As of 2026, average monthly parking costs: New York ($500+), San Francisco ($400), Boston ($350), Chicago ($300), Seattle ($250), Denver ($200), Austin ($175). Prices vary by neighborhood and type of parking.
