How to Calculate Your Fuel Cost

The formula is simple: Fuel Cost = (Miles Driven ÷ MPG) × Gas Price

Example: If you drive 300 miles/week, your car gets 25 MPG, and gas costs $3.50/gallon:

  • Weekly gallons: 300 ÷ 25 = 12 gallons
  • Weekly cost: 12 × $3.50 = $42
  • Monthly cost: ~$182
  • Annual cost: ~$2,184

7 Ways to Save on Gas in 2026

  1. Use gas price apps — GasBuddy, Waze find cheapest stations near you
  2. Join grocery store fuel programs — Kroger, Safeway offer 5-10¢ off per gallon
  3. Drive at 55–65 MPH on highways — Optimal speed for fuel efficiency
  4. Keep tires inflated — Under-inflated tires waste 3% more fuel
  5. Remove roof racks when not in use — Reduces aerodynamic drag by 5-10%
  6. Combine errands into one trip — Warm engines are more efficient
  7. Consider a hybrid or EV — Save $1,000+/year on fuel

Factors That Affect Fuel Efficiency

  • Driving habits — Smooth acceleration and braking improve MPG by 10-20%
  • Vehicle maintenance — Properly inflated tires and regular oil changes help
  • Traffic conditions — Stop-and-go traffic burns 15-30% more fuel
  • Weather — Cold weather reduces MPG by 10-20%
  • AC usage — Running AC can reduce MPG by 5-10%

Frequently Asked Questions

How is fuel cost calculated?

Fuel cost = (Miles driven per year ÷ Fuel efficiency) × Gas price. For monthly costs, divide the annual figure by 12.

What is a good fuel efficiency rating?

For gas-powered cars: 25 MPG city / 30+ MPG highway is average. 40+ MPG is excellent. Hybrids typically get 50+ MPG.

How can I improve my fuel efficiency?

Drive at consistent speeds, avoid rapid acceleration, keep tires properly inflated, remove excess weight, and use cruise control on highways.
