Florida Home Insurance Overview

Florida homeowners insurance averages $4,960/year ($413/month), which is well above the national average of $2,230/year. Florida rates are driven by hurricane and flood risk.

  • Average annual premium: $4,960
  • Average monthly: $413
  • Per $1,000 coverage: $16.14
  • Median home value: $307,400
  • Florida rank: #2 most expensive state

What Florida Home Insurance Covers

  • Dwelling: Physical structure of your home and attached structures
  • Other structures: Detached garage, shed, fence (typically 10% of dwelling coverage)
  • Personal property: Furniture, electronics, clothing (50-70% of dwelling)
  • Loss of use: Hotel and living expenses if home is uninhabitable (20-30% of dwelling)
  • Liability: Legal protection if someone is injured on your property ($100K-$300K standard)
  • Medical payments: Small medical bills for guests injured on your property ($1K-$5K)

Florida-Specific Insurance Risks

  • Hurricanes: Major risk — wind and flood damage. Separate flood insurance required (FEMA NFIP or private)
  • Flood zones: Check FEMA flood maps — flood insurance is NOT included in standard policies
  • Wind mitigation: {state} offers discounts for hurricane shutters, impact windows, and roof-to-wall connections

How to Save on Florida Home Insurance

  • Bundle home + auto: Save 10-25% on both policies with the same insurer
  • Raise your deductible: Going from $500 to $2,000 deductible can save 15-25%
  • Improve home security: Alarm systems, deadbolts, and smoke detectors save 5-15%
  • Maintain good credit: In most states, credit score impacts insurance rates significantly
  • Stay claim-free: 3-5 years without a claim earns a 10-20% discount
  • Shop every 2 years: Rates change constantly. Get 3-5 quotes each renewal cycle
  • Ask about Florida discounts: New roof, impact-resistant roofing, wind mitigation, loyalty

Frequently Asked Questions

How much is home insurance in Florida?

Average home insurance in Florida costs $4,960/year or $413/month. That's $16.14 per $1,000 of coverage. Rates vary by location, home age, and coverage level.

Is home insurance required in Florida?

Florida law does not require homeowners insurance, but your mortgage lender will require it. Even without a mortgage, insurance protects your largest asset from fire, storms, theft, and liability.
