30-Year vs 15-Year Mortgage: Which Is Better?

Choosing between a 30-year and 15-year mortgage is one of the biggest financial decisions you'll make. The trade-off is simple: lower monthly payments (30-year) vs massive interest savings (15-year).

Key Differences

Feature30-Year Fixed15-Year Fixed
Monthly PaymentLower~40-50% higher
Interest RateHigher (7.0% avg)Lower (6.2% avg)
Total Interest PaidMuch moreMuch less
Equity BuildupSlowFast
FlexibilityHigh (can pay extra)Low (locked in)
Tax DeductionMore interest to deductLess interest to deduct

The Hybrid Strategy: 30-Year + Extra Payments

One popular approach: take a 30-year mortgage but make extra payments as if it were a 15-year. You get the flexibility of lower required payments (helpful if income drops) while still saving on interest. The downside: your rate is higher than a true 15-year, so you'll pay slightly more interest than going with the 15-year directly.

When to Choose 30-Year

  • You're early in your career with growing income potential
  • You want to invest the difference in the stock market (historical ~8% returns vs ~7% mortgage rate)
  • You need lower payments to qualify for the loan
  • You plan to move within 7-10 years
  • You want the option to rent out the home later

When to Choose 15-Year

  • You're established in your career with stable income
  • You want to be debt-free before retirement
  • You're buying your "forever home"
  • You're close to retirement age
  • You prioritize guaranteed savings over market returns

Real Example: $300,000 Loan

At 7.0% for 30 years: monthly payment $1,996, total interest $418,560, total cost $718,560.

At 6.2% for 15 years: monthly payment $2,560, total interest $160,800, total cost $460,800.

Interest saved: $257,760 — but monthly payment is $564 higher.
