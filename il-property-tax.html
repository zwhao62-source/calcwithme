Illinois Property Tax Overview

Illinois has an effective property tax rate of 2.22%, making it one of the highest in the nation among all 50 states. The average annual property tax in Illinois is $5,370 on a median home value of $241,800.

  • Effective tax rate: 2.22%
  • Assessment ratio: 33.33%
  • Median home value: $241,800
  • Average annual tax: $5,370 ($448/month)

How Illinois Property Tax Works

Illinois properties are assessed at 33.33% of market value. The assessed value is then multiplied by the local millage rate to determine your annual tax bill.

Illinois Property Tax = Assessed Value × Local Millage Rate

Local millage rates vary by county, city, school district, and special districts within Illinois. Your total rate is the sum of all applicable levies.

Illinois Property Tax Exemptions

  • Homestead exemption: General homestead exemption up to $10,000 off equalized assessed value
  • Senior exemption: Available in most counties — check your local assessor's office for specific amounts and age thresholds (typically 65+)
  • Veteran exemption: Disabled veterans (10%+ rating) may qualify for additional exemptions — varies by county. Contact your county assessor for details
  • Disability exemption: Disability exemptions available in most counties — typically mirrors the senior exemption. Contact your county assessor for specific amounts

How to Appeal Your Illinois Property Tax

If you believe your Illinois property is over-assessed, you have the right to appeal:

  • 1. Contact your county assessor — Review your assessment for errors in square footage, lot size, or condition
  • 2. Gather comparable sales — Find 3-5 similar homes sold recently in your Illinois neighborhood with lower values
  • 3. File by the deadline — Illinois appeal deadlines vary by county; check your assessment notice
  • 4. Present evidence — Focus on facts and comparable data, not complaints about tax rates
  • 5. Success rate: 50-70% of Illinois property tax appeals result in a reduction
